Lesson 4: Lines

Lines are wonderful. You can do so much with lines. You can create

  • Forms, Shapes, Figures
  • Contours
  • Patterns
  • Shading
  • Perspective and space
  • Optical illusions

In this lesson we will look at the straight lines only.

  • You will learn

  • line
  • stroke
  • strokeWeight
  • position

Lines

You can create a straight with the line function.
You always need two points to create a line.

      line(x, y, x1, y1);

The line function has four params:

  • the first two params are the begin-position (x,y) of the line
  • the third and the fourth params are the end-position(x1,y1) position of the line.

Lines needs the same drawing tools as the point. You need a stroke function, so your drawingtool knows which color to use. You need a strokeWeight function, to tell your drawingtool how width your line should be.

The beginning and the end of the line can be set inside or outside the canvas.

In the next lesson as we learn more about variables you can start enjoying repeating lines on a smart way. Now you have to draw indidual lines, so make them special. 

        function setup(){
          createCanvas(300,300);
          background(100);
        }

        function draw(){
          stroke(240);
          strokeWeight(30);
          line(85, 130, 200,155);

          stroke(160);
          strokeWeight(20);
          line(50, 230, 200,-10);

          stroke(80);
          strokeWeight(10);
          line(40, 250, 240,160);

          stroke(0);
          strokeWeight(1);
          line(5, 290, 180,200);
          line(180,200, 100,20);
          line(100,20,5,290);
        }

Move your mouse over this canvas and draw a beautiful flower in the center of the canvas with your mouse.

  function setup(){
    createCanvas(300,300);
    background(100);
  }

  function draw(){
    stroke(255);
    strokeWeight(0.5);
    line(width/2,height/2,mouseX, mouseY);
  }

In the second example there are two more tricks. I started the line in the center of the canvas. You can find the center of a canvas by 

        x-position of the center of the canvas = width/2;
        y-position of the center of the canvas = height/2;

After you create a canvas, p5 stores the width of the canvas in the variable width. and the height of the canvas in the variable height.

Two other variables of p5 are mouseX and mouseY. p5 stores the position of the mouse in these two variables.

You will learn more about variables in the next lesson.
