Ellipses and Circles

If you want to draw an ellipse, you have to know it's position and the width and height of the ellipse. 

        ellipse(x, y, ellipsewidth, ellipseheight);
The x, and y position are at the center of your ellipse and when the ellipsewidth and ellipseheight are the same you will have a circle.

Before you can draw an ellipse, you have to prepare some drawingtools. You know the stroke and the strokeWeight function they are used to draw the borderline of the ellipse. The fill function is used for the fillcolor. 

      fill(200);
    
function setup(){
  createCanvas(300,300);
  background(100);
  stroke(0);
  strokeWeight(2);
  fill(200);
  ellipse(150,200, 200, 100);
}
function draw(){
  stroke(0);
  strokeWeight(1);
  fill(255);
  ellipse(random(100,200),random(100,200),10,10);
}

Rectangles and Squares

Rectangles and squares are drawn almost the same as Ellises and Circles. First you need a stroke, strokeWeight and a fill function. 

        stroke(255);
        strokeWeight(5);
        fill(50);
        rect(x,y, rectwidth, rectheight);

But there is a difference, the rectangle is not drawn from the center of the rectangle, but from the top-left-corner of the rectangle.



open sketch in openprocessing 


function setup(){
  createCanvas(300,300);
  background(100);
}

function draw(){
  stroke(255);
  strokeWeight(2);
  fill(0);
  rect(0,0,width/3, height/3 );
  rect(width/3, height/3, width/3*2, height/3*2 );
}
